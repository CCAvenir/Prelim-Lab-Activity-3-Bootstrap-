About Vincent van Gogh

Who is Vincent van Gogh?

Vincent van Gogh (1853-1890) was a Dutch post-impressionist painter, known for his vivid colors, emotional depth, and bold brushwork. Despite creating over 2,100 artworks, including around 860 oil paintings, he was virtually unknown during his lifetime and struggled with mental illness. Today, van Gogh is one of the most influential figures in the history of Western art.

His paintings are characterized by expressive brushstrokes, emotional intensity, and a unique use of color. Van Gogh's style evolved significantly, moving through periods of realism, impressionism, and a final, signature post-impressionistic style.

Van Gogh's Iconic Paintings

Van Gogh's paintings are known for their emotional intensity and vibrant colors. Some of his most famous works include:

  • The Starry Night (1889): One of his most recognized works, depicting a swirling night sky over a small town.
  • Sunflowers (1888): A series of paintings featuring vibrant sunflowers in a vase, capturing the beauty of nature in bright yellow hues.
  • The Bedroom (1888): A depiction of van Gogh's bedroom in Arles, symbolizing peace and solitude.
  • Irises (1889): A vibrant floral composition that van Gogh painted while recovering in a mental asylum.
